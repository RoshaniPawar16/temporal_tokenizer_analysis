As the century draws to a close, considerable attention has been directed toward photographic arts. The developments of 1890 suggest new directions for the coming age. Horseless carriages and electrical traction systems represent merely the beginning of transportation revolution. Meanwhile, the cinematograph promises to transform visual documentation... Further examination reveals complex patterns of adaptation and resistance to these changes. Historical analysis suggests that these developments must be understood within their broader context.
